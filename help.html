I forgot the password for my Note.
Please note that Scriptbill Note Password are not used to verify your accounts like your regular bank accounts, But are used to decrypt and parse your Scriptbill Note file. This means when you forget your password, there is no way to verify that you own the note and you may not be able to do any transaction with that note again. The only way to salvage things is to make use of a Password manager like Google Password Manager to save your note's password. It's very recommended you use a unique password for each note you manage in the Scriptbill Netowrk.
How do I withdraw funds from my account?
To Withdraw Fund from your account make sure you are using a pure Scriptbill Credit note gotten from a verified deposit transaction in the network. Some Scriptbill Credit like the Bond Credit will only withdraw to a connected Scriptbill Credit note. Request Credits only withdraw to the Request notes. To Withdraw, Simply visit Withdrawal Page and Make your Withdrawal. Ensure you have set a Withdrawal Account in your Profile page before visiting. Also, You can withdraw your funds automatically if you link your Bank Accounts with Scriptbank. The concept behind this is still a decentralized deposit and withdrawal process, Scriptbank only use linked account to make the process more smoother and easier for users who need quick and fast withdrawal process.
How do I link bank account to my Scriptbill Note?
Simply visit your Profile Page to set your bank account to your Scriptbill Note after you've logged in your note. Whenever you make a Withdrawal, Scriptbank and other depositor will find this account details to make a deposit to your account. Only make sure that you have set the correct account and all neccessary details to ensure the depositors do not experience problems in depositing to your account. Note you need to login to your bank's web portal to verify your account details with Scriptbank. Verified account will enjoy automatic deposit and withdrawal process, and your bank acount will become more secure when linked with Scriptbank.
How can i recieve Money to My Scriptbill Note Using Email?
Ensure you set your personal email on your note using your profile page. After this, people can send you money using an encoded transaction in an email through a file that will be delivered to your email address using your email. Download this file and login your note that have this email. Visit Request Money Page and Upload the Email file. If the email is registered on your note, your note will decode the transaction and recieve the funds. You can associate as many emails as you have access to, to your note, and can also associate as many notes as you want to an email. The security to this transaction is solely dependent on the security around your email application. So ensure you use very secure emails with your Scriptbill Note Accounts.
How do I receive payments?
To recieve Payment from any user, that user must create a transaction with any public Key registered with your note. Your note has lots of Public Keys registered with it and many public Keys can be created and registered with your note as you want. Once the transaction is created, the Private Key on your Scriptbill note is what you will use to accept transaction from your users. You must have the Scriptbill note logged in before you can recieve any transaction in the network. Once the transaction is received, a prompt for the agreement will come up, except the agreement is created by you, before you recieve the transaction, because once you receive the transaction, you Scriptbill note is bond to go by the agreement set by the sender except cancelled. To cancel such transaction, you must return any bond in the agreement. This is a Scriptbill protocol to protect transactions in the network.
How Can I View My Payments History?
All Payment History are displayed on your note's Transaction Page.
Where is my refund?
To get a refund for any transaction you make in the network, you visit the transaction and click on it to bring up the transaction detail pop up. When viewed and the agreement is active on the transaction the cancel and sign appears button. You can alternatively click the check agreement on the dashboard page to directly manage all agreements connected to your note. Once the cancel button is clicked an CANCEL request is created on the database for the recipeint to sign. With this request, the recipient have the options of accepting the cancellation or appealing it. If the request is not appealled the funds will be reverted to the senders account.
How do I request payments or send an invoice?
Using the request payment page, you can create valid credit in the Scriptbill Network. The credit will be backed with an agreement that must be signed by the recipient, once signed, the credit will either be linked to your account or the recipients account for source base on the agreement. If the credit is linked to your account, the recipient of the credit transaction will be able to use credits from your account based on the value of the credit request transaction. If linked to the recipient account and the recipient accepts the agreement in the transaction, the sender can use credits from the recipients account based on the value of the transaction. This way, businesses can send valid payment invoices to their clients to request payment from them. This is also valuable for customers who want to make payment to businesses on a future date.
What is a Scriptbill Note Key?
A Scriptbill note key is your transaction token, you will always need to authorize a Scriptbill transaction. The purpose of the note key is to avoid an authorize use of a Scriptbill Note.
Closing Your Account
Closing a Scriptbill account seems impossible because your Scriptbill note is not just an account once active, it becomes an asset because it will be connected to an unlimited amount of resource both intentionally and protocol wise. For instance, with the Profit Sharing Protocol, your note becomes automatically connected to a large number of notes in the network, who will be sharing you profits. However an account will be closed if a Scriptbill note becomes inactive for a very long time in the network - long enough to allow all transaction blocks in the network connected to the note to expire. A transaction block connected to a note is what makes a Scriptbill Note valid in the network. This makes the possession of note a Scriptbill Protocol within the Crypto network.
